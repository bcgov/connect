## 🌟 What the Platform Offers You

By onboarding your program area onto the Connect Platform, you instantly unlock a suite of vetted **Common Capabilities**. You don’t need to build these from scratch; you simply configure and plug into them:


### 👤 1. Identity & Account Management (BCSC Portal)
Secure, instant access for individuals, businesses, and staff.

The Connect Platform is a fully integrated **BC Services Card (BCSC) portal**. Instead of building complex, custom identity brokers, you instantly gain high-assurance, secure access pathways for citizens, businesses, and staff:

* **BC Services Card (BCSC):** Tap into trusted, high-assurance citizen identity verification, allowing users to log in securely using their mobile BCSC
* **BCeID:** Provide reliable low-to-medium assurance authentication for businesses and individual accounts
* **IDir:** Enable seamless internal access for B.C. Public Service employees and administrative staff

### 💳 2. Payment & Financial Management (PayBC & Beyond)

Say goodbye to complex merchant accounts and financial reconciliation audits:

* Handled statutory filings, revenue collection, and electronic disbursements.


* Automatic daily disbursements to internal ministries via **Electronic Journal Voucher (EJV)** general ledger (GL) routing.


* Daily disbursements to broader public sector partners (such as Crown Corporations) via **Corporate Accounting System (CAS)** supplier records.


* Full support for Pre-Authorized Debits (PAD), Credit Cards, Online Banking, and EFT.



### 🎨 3. Consistent Design & Nuxt Components

Build beautiful, accessible frontends in half the time:

* **Standardized UI/UX Patterns:** Standardized components leveraging Nuxt and following the **Digital Code of Practice** to ensure a highly familiar, intuitive experience for British Columbians.


* **Reusable Code Wiki:** Access pre-configured components for layout, form validation, user authentication, and office address flows.



### 📞 4. Omni-Channel Support Ecosystem

Your users aren't left stranded on a website:

* Integrated support spanning digital self-service, real-time web chat, telephone helplines, and physically staffed Service BC windows.

## 🗺️ The 4-Step Onboarding Framework

Onboarding is highly standardized to minimize customizations and maintain a "Common by Default" approach. Here is your roadmap to going live:

![flow diagram of choices](/img/product-owners/flow1.png)

### 🏁 Step 1: Decisions

Determine your scope:

* **New Build?** (e.g., *Short-Term Rental Registry*).


* **Updating an Existing App?** (e.g., migrating an legacy application to use modernization components like *Sites Registry*).



### 🛠️ Step 2: Confirm Tech & Components

Work with our team to map out the required common capabilities:

* Will your app reside in the unified Connect digital catalog (enabling users to manage accounts, services, and billing under one single dashboard)?


* Or will your app remain standalone, simply consuming the application identity and payment API components?



### 🗣️ Step 3: Confirm Service Delivery Channels

Map out the citizen journey:

* Define how digital self-serve maps to phone help desks, live chat, or Service BC walk-in locations to support an inclusive, accessible rollout.



### 📝 Step 4: Finalize Agreements & Implement

Establish operational readiness:

* Finalize memorandums of understanding (MOUs).
* Align on service fee frameworks ($1.50 / $1.00 transaction allocations) to support continuous platform optimization.


* Complete security, privacy (PIA), and technical compliance checks to move to production.
